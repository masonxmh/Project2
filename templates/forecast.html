Comparison between SARS and ConV

According to health experts, the continued spread of Coronavirus is "more infectious" but less severe than the SARS epidemic in late 2002, a
coronavirus named SARS erupted in southern China, causing severe pneumonia that rapidly spread to other countries. It infected more than
8,000 people and killed 774 -- and then it disappeared.
In 2012, another coronavirus dubbed MERS began sickening people in Saudi Arabia. It’s still hanging around, causing small numbers of
infections each year. The World Health Organization has counted nearly 2,500 cases of MERS in the Middle East and beyond, and more than
850 deaths.
...
However, it took eight months for SARS to spread to more than 8,000 people. The Wuhan coronavirus infected over 20,000 people in
about five weeks.We’ve known about this particular virus shortly after a cluster of severe pneumonia cases were reported on New Year’s
Eve in Wuhan, which is in the Hubei Province of China. On January 9, virologists and other public health researchers identified the strain as a
novel coronavirus, which was tied to a specific “wet market” in the city of Wuhan where they sell fish and other live animals.
Until now, the new 2019 coronavirus has been spreaded to 25 countries. The disease is spreading quickly. China’s health minister, Ma Xiaowei,
told reporters last month that there is evidence it’s already mutated into a stronger variation that is able to spread more easily among humans.
World health officials know the respiratory disease is capable of spreading through human-to-human contact, droplets carried through sneezing
and coughing, and germs left on inanimate objects. The illness is cable of spreading before symptoms show, and about 20% of patients become
severely ill, leading to pneumonia and respiratory failure, health officials say.

Forecasts

As for forecasting, based on the data we have now about the novel 2019 coronavirus, which outbreaked in Wuhan, China, we did a forecast for the next 10 days using the ARIMA model in R to forecast the daily confirmed cases in Hubei,China (which is the province Wuhan city is located), ... as well as China as the country where the new virus has been confirmed in the first place.
As the original data is not stationary, our forecast was based on the first difference of the original data in order to achieve stationarity.
According to our forecast, there will be no sharp increase or decrease in the number of daily confirmed cases in Hubei and China as a whole. However, there are still several observable peaks in terms of numbers. Also, we see no trends of decreasing in numbers. This way, we could predict that the coronavirus will still be a serious case, the number of confirmed cases will still be increasing in China but at a slower rate comparing to the past days since the 2019 Coronavirus outbreaks.
